Every website is made of sections that display content and help orient users. Sections can be designd in a variety of ways and combined with other sections to create a unique page. The Landing Page has 5 sections, which can be used to display a variety of content, ranging from long-form text to image galleries.

Not sure what a website section is? Check out this reference sheet for a guided tour of each section type.

The navigation bar usually appears at the top of the page, though some websites are intentionally designed with it at the bottom.

Navigation bars typically display a logo or brand name, along with a few links to different parts of the website. The links may collapse when viewed on a mobile, but remain accessible through a menu icon.

The masthead is usually found at the top of a page, displaying an image, logo, or slogan. Mastheads range in size and design, with the primary goal being to grab attention.

Animations
& Interactions.

Animations cause pieces of your website to move, change shape, or otherwise transform. When used judiciously, they can engage the audience, create flow, and provide a feedback when a user interacts with the site by scrolling, clicking, or tapping.

User interactions are pieces of code that allow a user to interact with pieces of the website directly. They often involve animations of some sort, but can also be something like a contact form.

Hamburger menu

A classic user interaction, hamburger menus have become ubiquitous in mobile navigation bar design. You'll most often see them at the top of a website, aligned to the right. When you tap them, they open a menu of links to other parts of the website.
